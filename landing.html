How it works (MVP)

1) Add invoices (manual, or CSV import). Track due date + last follow‑up.

2) Open your chase list. AR Copilot tells you who needs a follow‑up today and why.

3) Copy the message (subject + body). Send it however you already send email.

4) Click “Mark sent”. You stay consistent without keeping mental notes.

  • Choose a tone: friendly / firm
  • Optional late fee wording toggle
  • Remind them of payment methods + link

Message examples

These are short on purpose. You’re not begging—you’re running a business.

1 day overdue
Subject: Quick check — Invoice #1843

Hi {{ClientName}},
Just checking in—Invoice #1843 for ${{Amount}} was due {{DueDate}}. Can you confirm it’s in process and share an ETA for payment?

Thanks,
{{YourName}}
7 days overdue (firm)
Subject: Action needed — Invoice #1843 is past due

Hi {{ClientName}},
Invoice #1843 (${{Amount}}) is now 7 days past due. Please confirm the payment date by EOD today so we can keep the project schedule on track.

If there’s a billing issue, reply here and I’ll resolve it quickly.

— {{YourName}}
14 days overdue (final notice)
Subject: Final notice — Invoice #1843

Hi {{ClientName}},
Invoice #1843 (${{Amount}}) remains unpaid 14 days past due. Please arrange payment by {{Date+3}}.

If payment isn’t received by then, we’ll pause work and move this to our collections process.

Thanks,
{{YourName}}

FAQ

Do I have to connect QuickBooks/Xero?

No. MVP starts with manual entry or CSV import. Integrations come later.

Will this send emails for me?

Not in the first version. We focus on copy‑paste + logging first (fast, low‑risk). Later: Gmail/O365 send with approval + templates.

Is this legal advice / collections?

No. AR Copilot provides messaging templates and workflow support. You control what you send.

What if I have clients who are “sensitive”?

That’s why we support tone settings and short, professional language. You can keep it friendly without being vague.
